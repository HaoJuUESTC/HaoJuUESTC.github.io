A Brief Introduction

I was a full-stack Software Development Engineer at Amazon Canada from Feb 2022 to Mar 2023, developing the Amazon Marketing Cloud Premium Subscription Service. Prior to that, I worked as a support researcher at the Human-Machine Interaction Lab, Huawei 2012 labs (their R&D department), working on smart home appliances from April 2021 to Jan 2022.

I received a research-based Master's degree from School of Information Studies, McGill University, with a focus on Human Computer Interaction (HCI), accessibility & usability, and wearable devices. My supervisor was Prof. Karyn Moffatt. I also worked with National Research Council to develop data visualization visualization tools for people from non-technical backgrounds. I graduated May 2021.

Prior to joining McGill University in 2019 as a master's student, I was a full-time research assistant at City University of Hong Kong, conducting research with Dr. Kening Zhu on Human Computer Interaction, with a special interest in tangible interfaces, haptics and usability. I designed, developed and enhanced hardware & firmware prototypes.

I received my Bachelor's degree in Electronic Engineering from School of Electronic Engineering, University of Electronic Science and Technology of China with distinction in June 2018. My thesis, Target Recognition and Tracking based on XGBoost, was accepted as a poster prsentation for IET International Radar Conference 2018

As an undergrad, I worked in Prof. Jianyu Yang’s Radar System and Signal Processing team as a student researcher under the supervision of Prof. Wei Yi, investigating Real-Time Target Detection and Estimation.

I was a former Mitacs Glibalink Intern at McGill University. I worked at Shared Reality Lab, supervised by Prof. Jeremy Cooperstock. Our research designing and investigating the usability of a multi-functional foot-based musical interface went later as a full paper in ACM SIGCHI Conference on Designing Interactive Systems (DIS) 2018.

I look forward to working with other enthusiastic researchers and engineers, designing & enhancing prototypes that will bring more convenience into our life :)

    Research Experience

    Here are some of my major research projects, in chronological order:

    Limb-Based Interactive System for Older Adults

    Designing and developing a system to perform hand activity sensing with wearable millimeter wave sensor TI IWR 1448 and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) algorithm. Currently building the data collection pipeline.

    Limb-Based Interactive System for Older Adults

    Sept. 2020--Present
    McGill University, Montreal

    Designing, building, and troubleshooting the firmware and hardware of a wearable limb-based input system for older adults from scratch, using Arduino and Python.

    Due to COVID-19, we used a Wizard-of-Oz method, so that the experiment can be conducted remotely by mailing experiment materials to the participants' home contact-free. Built the mats embedded with pressure sensors to track foot movement using Arduino; Designed and built the interface displayed on screen using Python TkInter and PySerial.

    Collecting and analysing performance data using the prototype and System Usability Questionnaire to qualitatively and quantitatively analyse the difference in interaction patterns, performances, and user preferences between older adults and their younger peers.

    CodeRhythm: Designing Inclusive Tangible Programming Blocks

    Oct. 2018--May 2019
    City University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong

    Inspired by "Scratch", we designed and prototyed a musical building block system in the setting of clasroom teaching for visually impaired schoolchildren, using Arduino. This project aims to introduce the basic programming ideas (such as variables and programming sequences -- loop, switch and sequential order, etc.) through the tangible, multi-sensory programming media. System developer. Codes availlable here

    Thermal-Based Displaying Device for the Visually Impaired

    Aug. 2018--Oct.2018
    City University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong

    We prototyped a thermal-based displaying device, set in the context of geographical education for the visually impaired schoolchildren. We are planning to investigate its usability (especially in picture displaying) compared with traditional Brailles.

    Target Recognition and Tracking based on XGBoost

    Oct.2017-May 2018
    UESTC, Chengdu, China

    Developed a supervised learning based target tracking algorithm and estimated its performance versus traditional target tracking algorithms (filtering algorithm: Kalman, LSM, target co-relating algorithms: JPDA, NNJPDA). Implemented in MATLAB and Python

    Alogrithm implemented in MATLAB and Python.

    Paper accepted as poster presentation in IET International Radar Conference 2018.

    Raising the Heat

    Sept.2017-Oct.2017
    McGill University, Montreal, Canada

    A following-up research of the UIST Student Innovation Contest 2016 project "Raising the Heat", where the possibility of using Electro-Muscular Stimulation to simulate a burning-hot temperature was explored. Hardware and firmware prototype was enhanced for more precise experiment results.

    Usability of Multi-Functional Foot-based Interfaces

    July 2017-Sept. 2017
    McGill University, Montreal, Canada

    In this research, we compared the performance of two mainstream foot interaction methods (foot rocking and heel-pivoted rotation) in selection and parameter controlling tasks, set in the use case of a hands-free interface designed for seated musicians.

    Implemented hardware design, co-designed and conducted experiments, and co-authored the paper (second author).

    Currently a paper in Proceedings of ACM SIGCHI Conference on Designing Interactive Systems (DIS) 2018.

    Full paper available here.

    Digital Signal Generation and Transmission System

    June 2017
    UESTC, Chengdu, China

    Course project of "Communication Theory and Systems", where a random signal source was generated, and later transmitted, received and de-modulated in 2 different means of modulation (in my case ASK & LFM). The waveform during the whole process, in time and frequency domain, was plotted. Matched filtering was introduced in the receiving process.

    SNR, gate threshold (when judging if the signal is "1" or "0" during receiving) self definable, and their influence in Pe were discussed.

    Sampling rate, carrier frequency, numbers of generated signals, etc. all self definable.

    Original MATLAB code and design report here.

    Data Secured USB Mass Storage Device

    Mar.2017-Sept. 2017
    UESTC, Chengdu, China

    Participated in the development of a data secured USB Flash Disk capable of data encrypting, sending anti-losing alerts and data retrieving. Hardware developer.

    This project ranked 7th among 125 teams in the provincial final of the National `Internet Plus' Innovation and Entrepreneurship Competition.

    Project report (in Chinese) availiable here.

    Smart Pet-Keeping Kit for Arthropods and Amphibians, Based on Single-Chip and Network Interface

    Aug.2016-May 2017
    UESTC, Chengdu, China

    Co-developed a MCU-based intelligent pet-keeping kit under automatic temperature and humidity control, with manual control over network interface.

    Chief researcher and system designer. Designed, implemented the hardware system and programmed the 8051 microcontroller

    Design report (in Chinese) and codes (in C) availiable here.

    Network-based RF Device Analyzer

    Mar.2016-July 2016
    UESTC, Chengdu, China

    Co-developed a network-based RF device analyzer with RF bandwidth of 4 GHz and baseband bandwidth of 125 MHz based on Xilinx Virtex VC707. GUI programmer, aided with hardware design.

    Capable of performing verification of algorithm on linear digital pre-distortion as well as establishing behavioral model of RF amplifiers apart from characteristic analyzing.

    This project was awarded second prize of Southwest China Area in OpenHW 2016 National College Student 'Smarter Connected' System Innovation Competition.

    Design report and demo (password 349), both in Chinese, are availiable.

    Amplitude Modulation Broadcast System based on Simulink Toolbox

    May 2016
    UESTC, Chengdu, China

    Course project of Signals and Systems. A design and simulation of a multi-channel analog communication system based on AM broadcast system Simulink toolbox, capable of reading and generating graphs (in time & frequency domain) regarding the audio input, with noise intensity and damping rate changeable. The effects of noise intensity and damping rate changeable on the system performance were later discussed.

    MATLAB and Simulink documents availiable.
